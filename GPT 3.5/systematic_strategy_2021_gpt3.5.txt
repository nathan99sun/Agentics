SYSTEMATIC TRADING RULES
===========================================

Data Exploration Summary:
- The data for December 2020 consists of 497 firms with standardized characteristics: mve, bm, and mom12m.
- Extreme values for mve, bm, and mom12m were identified, with some firms exhibiting very high or very low z-scores.
- Testing different conditions showed limited overlap in extreme values for bm and mom12m.

BUY RULE:
if bm > 0.5 AND mom12m > 0.5 AND mve > 0.5:
    signal = BUY

SELL RULE:
if bm < -1.5 OR mom12m < -1.0:
    signal = SELL

HOLD RULE:
else:
    signal = HOLD

Rationale:
- BUY Rule: The BUY rule focuses on selecting firms with relatively higher values of bm, mom12m, and mve. This combination may indicate undervalued firms with positive momentum and size.
- SELL Rule: The SELL rule targets firms with very low bm or poor momentum, suggesting overvalued or weak-performing stocks.
- HOLD Rule: The HOLD rule serves as the default option for firms that do not meet the specific BUY or SELL conditions.

Expected signal distribution:
- Based on the tested conditions, the expected distribution would result in a relatively small percentage of firms meeting the BUY or SELL criteria, with the majority falling into the HOLD category.
