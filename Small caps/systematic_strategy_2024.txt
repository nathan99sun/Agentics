===========================================
SYSTEMATIC TRADING RULES
===========================================

Data Exploration Summary:
- The data includes standardized book-to-market (bm), momentum (mom12m), and size (mve) characteristics for a set of firms.
- All characteristics are z-scores (standard deviations from the mean).
- Extreme values (above 2 or below -2) exist for all characteristics.
- I observed that many firms have simultaneously extreme values across multiple characteristics.

BUY RULE:
if bm > 0.75 AND mom12m > 0.5 AND mve > 0:
    signal = BUY

SELL RULE:
if bm < -0.75 AND mom12m < -0.5:
    signal = SELL

HOLD RULE:
else:
    signal = HOLD

Rationale:
- BUY Rule: This rule targets undervalued companies (high bm) with positive momentum (high mom12m) and a preference for larger firms (mve > 0). The economic rationale is that undervalued stocks tend to revert to their mean, and positive momentum suggests continued outperformance. The size filter adds a safety net.
- SELL Rule: This rule targets overvalued companies (low bm) with negative momentum (low mom12m). The economic rationale is that overvalued stocks are likely to correct downwards, and negative momentum suggests continued underperformance.
- Expected Signal Distribution: I expect the BUY and SELL signals to be relatively infrequent, targeting only the most extreme cases. The majority of firms will likely be assigned a HOLD signal.
